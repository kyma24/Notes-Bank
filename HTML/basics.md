-   The 5 Greatest Books on the Bookshelf
    
    ```html
    <!DOCTYPE html>
    <html>
    <head>
    <title>The 5 Greatest Books on the Bookshelf</title>
    </head>
    <body>
    
    <h1>The 5 Greatest Books on the Bookshelf</h1>
    <p><i>compiled by Kimi Ma</i></p>
    
    <br>
    
    <h2><ins>The Little Prince</ins></h2>
    <ol>
    <li>has many universal morals</li>
    <li>imaginative</li>
    <li>creative <del>great</del> illustrations</li>
    </ol>
    
    <hr>
    
    <p><i>by <mark>Antoine de Saint-Exupéry</mark><sup>1</sup>: A book about a boy from a different planet and his adventures across the galaxies, as told by the narrator, <b>a pilot</b> who crash-landed in a desert, where he discovers <strong>the Little Prince</strong>.</i></p>
    
    <br>
    <br>
    
    <h2><ins>Peril at End House</ins></h2>
    <ul>
    <li>ingeniously crafted</li>
    <li>great storyline</li>
    <li>surprising deduction</li>
    </ul>
    
    <hr>
    
    <p><i>by <mark>Agatha Christie</mark><sup>2</sup>: A detective by the name of <b>Hercule Poirot</b> meets a woman named <b>Magdala "Nick" Buckley</b>, and he believes that someone is out to kill her. It seems that no one has any motive. One night, when Nick holds a renunion party, her cousin, <b>Maggie</b>, is found dead with Nick's shawl. Who could be the murderer?</i></p>
    
    <br>
    <br>
    
    <h2><ins>Great Expectations</ins></h2>
    <ol>
    <li>slow storyline</li>
    <li>sad, moving ending</li>
    <li>complicated character relationships</li>
    </ol>
    
    <hr>
    
    <p><i>by <mark>Charles Dickens</mark>: Join <b>Pip</b> on his adventures as he climbs up the socioeconomic ranks from the age of 7, when he first meets <b>Miss Havisham</b> and her adopted daughter, <b>Estella</b>.</i></p>
    
    <br>
    <br>
    
    <h2><ins>Murder on the Orient Express</ins></h2>
    <ul>
    <li>shocking plot twists</li>
    <li>end-of-chapter cliff hangers</li>
    <li>suspense at its maximum</li>
    </ul>
    
    <hr>
    
    <p><i>by <mark>Agatha Christie</mark><sup>2</sup>: Detective <b>Hercule Poirot</b> gets a telegram from London requesting him back to solve a mystery. He meets all of his fellow passengers on the <b>Orient Express</b>, including a businessman by the name of <b>Ratchett</b>, who recognizes Poirot and asks for his protection, since he has been getting death threats. Poirot, repulsed by Ratchett's demeanor, rejects him. Strange and suspicious sounds ensue. The train has been stuck on the tracks because of a snowstorm, and the next morning, Ratchett is found dead.</i></p>
    
    <br>
    <br>
    
    <h2><ins>Thing Explainer</ins></h2>
    <ol>
    <li>complicated stuff in simple words</li>
    <li>intricately drawn diagrams</li>
    <li>minimal</li>
    </ol>
    
    <hr>
    
    <p><i>by <mark>Randall Munroe</mark><sup>3</sup>: This informational book contains detailed blueprints of various interesting objects, expressed with captions and diagrams containing <strong>only the 1000 most common words</strong>.</i></p>
    
    <br>
    <br>
    
    <p><small><sup>1</sup>Antoine de Saint-Exupéry was a man who felt that mankind was hopeless in their abilities to get along with others. He writes this story to illustrate his own thoughts about the lack of understanding in most adults of children and their thoughts.</small></p>
    
    <p><small><sup>2</sup>Agatha Christie has written many detective books in the course of her life, most of which feature the detective Hercule Poirot. Her books are the 3rd most sold of all time, behind Shakespeare and the Bible.</small></p>
    
    <p><small><sup>3</sup>Randall Munroe is an acclaimed webcomic artist and author. He has a blog named xkcd, primarily a stick figure comic. He has written the books <em>What If?</em>, <em>Thing Explainer</em>, and <em>How To</em>.</small></p>
    
    </body>
    </html>
    ```
